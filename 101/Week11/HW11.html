In this assignment, we will thoroughly analyze a portion of Anderson iris data which we've looked at in the past in the class. Recall that the iris data consists of observations of three species of iris (Iris setosa, Iris versicolor and Iris virginica). The length and width of petals and sepals. Here, we will look at the relationship between sepal length and sepal width.

1) Regression

Model the sepal length (Y) with respect to sepal width (X) using a linear regression of the form: \[ M1: Y_i = \alpha + \beta X_i + \epsilon_i \] where \( i \in (1,2,...N) \) is the index of each observation and \( \epsilon_i \) are i.i.d. random variables with distribution \( {\cal N}(0,\sigma^2) \). Follow these steps:

a) Plot the sepal length against sepal width.

b) Fit a regression model, creating an object called (for example) Iris.lm1, and report the estimates for \( \alpha \), \( \beta \) and \( \sigma \) in your model. Illustrate this regression by adding a line to your plot.

c) Assess using diagnostic plots whether the assumptions of i.i.d. normally distributed \( \epsilon \) are valid.

d) Test via ANOVA the hypothesis that there is a significant relationship between the two variables. Identify the hypothesis you are testing with respect to the parameters defined above, report the ANOVA table and the p-value, and conclude whether there is a relationship between the two based on the results of the ANOVA.

2) One-Way ANOVA

Now, propose a new model the sepal length (Y), as a function of the discrete species factor (A): \[ M2: Y_{ij} = \mu_i + \epsilon_{ij} \] where \( i \in (1,2,3) \) represents each of the three species, \( j \in (1,2, ... 50) \) represents the replicates of each species, and \( \epsilon_i \) are i.i.d. \( {\cal N}(0,\sigma^2) \) random variables.

a) Plot the sepal length against species with a boxplot.

b) Produce a summary table of means and standard deviations for each species.

c) Fit a linear model of sepal length with respect to species.

d) Assess whether the assumptions of i.i.d. normally distributed \( \epsilon \) are valid using diagnostic plots.

e) Test via ANOVA the hypothesis that there is a significant relationship between these variables. Identify the hypothesis you are testing with respect to the parameters defined above, report the ANOVA table and p-value, and conclude whether there is a relationship between the two.

3) Two-Way ANOVA (aka ANCOVA)

Let us propose two models that include both main effects and interaction effects.

\[ M3: Y_{ij} = \alpha_i + \beta X_{ij} + \epsilon_{ij} \] \[ M4: Y_{ij} = \alpha_i + \beta_i X_{ij} + \epsilon_{ij} \]

Where \( \alpha_i \) represents a unique intercept for each species \( i \) and \( \beta_i \) (in M4) represents a unique slope for each species \( i \).

a) How many parameters are needed to specify each of these two models? Name them.

b) Fit both of these models (creating, e.g., objects Iris.lm3 and Iris.lm4), and report the values of all the parameter estimates.

c) Illustrate both of these models using color-coded regression lines superposed on a scatter plot.

d) Use an ANOVA table to compare these models. What hypothesis are you testing in making this comparison? What model best explains the observed variation in sepal length?
